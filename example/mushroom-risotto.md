# Mushroom Risotto (TM6)

**Day 2 (tomorrow) — Serves 2**

---

## Ingredients

- 250g Carnaroli risotto rice
- 485g chestnut mushrooms, cleaned
- 1 onion, halved
- 2 garlic cloves
- 50g Parmigiano Reggiano, cut into chunks
- 40g unsalted butter (split: 20g + 20g)
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- 100ml dry white wine
- 750ml hot water + 1 chicken stock cube (dissolved)
- Fresh thyme leaves (a few sprigs)
- Salt & pepper

### To serve
- Truffle oil (a few drops per bowl)
- Fresh parsley, chopped
- Remaining Parmigiano

---

## Method

### Step 1 — Grate the parmesan
Add 50g Parmigiano chunks to TM bowl.
**Grate: 10 sec / Speed 10**

Set aside in a bowl.

### Step 2 — Chop and brown mushrooms
Add 485g mushrooms to TM bowl (quarter any large ones first).
**Chop: 3 sec / Speed 4**

Remove. Heat a large pan over high heat with a little butter or oil. Sauté the mushrooms in batches — don't crowd the pan. Cook until well browned, ~5–7 min total. Season lightly. Set aside.

### Step 3 — Chop onion and garlic
Add to TM bowl:
- 1 onion (halved)
- 2 garlic cloves

**Chop: 5 sec / Speed 7**

Scrape down.

### Step 4 — Saute the base
Add 20g butter and 1 tbsp olive oil.
**Cook: 3 min / 120°C / Speed 1**

### Step 5 — Toast the rice
Add 250g risotto rice.
**Cook: 2 min / 120°C / Reverse / Speed Soft Stir**

### Step 6 — Deglaze
Add 100ml dry white wine.
**Cook: 2 min / 100°C / Reverse / Speed Soft Stir**

### Step 7 — Add stock and mushrooms
Add to TM bowl:
- 750ml hot stock (1 chicken stock cube dissolved in 750ml hot water)
- The browned mushrooms
- A few sprigs of fresh thyme leaves

**Cook: 18 min / 100°C / Reverse / Speed Soft Stir**

Check at 16 min — rice should be al dente with a creamy consistency. Add a splash more hot water if too thick; cook 1–2 min more if needed.

### Step 8 — Mantecatura (finishing)
Add to TM bowl:
- 20g butter
- Most of the grated Parmigiano (save some for serving)

**Mix: 30 sec / Reverse / Speed Soft Stir**

Season with salt and pepper.

---

## Serve
In warm bowls. Top with remaining Parmigiano, fresh parsley, a drizzle of truffle oil, and a crack of black pepper.

---

## Notes
- Truffle oil: a little goes a long way — just a few drops per bowl.
